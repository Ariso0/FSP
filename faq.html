Frequently Asked Questions

What is Save Our Species Malaysia?

Save Our Species Malaysia is a local conservation initiative dedicated to protecting Malaysia’s endangered wildlife and natural habitats. We aim to secure a sustainable future for our biodiversity and inspire future generations to become environmental stewards.

Why is wildlife conservation important in Malaysia?

Malaysia is one of the world’s biodiversity hotspots, home to unique species like the Malayan tiger, orangutan, tapir, and hornbill. These species play vital roles in our ecosystem, and their survival ensures environmental balance for future generations.

How are Malaysian species threatened?

  • Deforestation and habitat loss
  • Illegal poaching and wildlife trade
  • Pollution and plastic waste
  • Climate change
  • Human-wildlife conflict

How does this initiative benefit future generations?

By preserving wildlife today, we:

  • Maintain natural ecosystems that support clean water, food, and air
  • Provide future Malaysians with the opportunity to experience native wildlife
  • Encourage youth to lead in environmental protection and innovation

How can students and youth get involved?

We offer:

  • Eco-education workshops and school outreach
  • Youth ambassador programs
  • Volunteer opportunities and eco-camps
  • Art, video, and essay contests on conservation themes

Can teachers and schools collaborate with you?

Yes! We welcome collaboration with educators to:

  • Integrate conservation topics into school curricula
  • Organize talks, tree-planting, or awareness events
  • Provide field trip experiences to nature reserves or wildlife centers

Where does funding go?

Funds support:

  • Habitat restoration and reforestation
  • Anti-poaching patrols and wildlife monitoring
  • Community engagement and education
  • Youth-focused conservation programs

Can I donate or adopt an endangered animal?

Yes! Symbolic adoptions help support field work, and you’ll receive an adoption pack with an e-certificate, species facts, and regular updates on your impact.

What are some of the key species you're helping protect?

  • Malayan tiger
  • Bornean orangutan
  • Sunda pangolin
  • Proboscis monkey
  • River terrapin

How can I stay updated or reach out?

Follow us on social media or contact us through our website. Subscribe to our newsletter for news, educational content, and updates on upcoming youth programs and conservation projects.

How do I sign up to volunteer?

You can sign up by filling out our Volunteer Sign-In Form. It only takes a few minutes! Once registered, you'll receive updates about upcoming activities, such as:

  • Tree planting
  • Clean-up campaigns
  • Wildlife monitoring
  • School visits and awareness events

Note: Volunteers of all ages are welcome, but minors will need parental consent.
